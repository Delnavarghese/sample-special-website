Special Olympics

The Special Olympics is an international sports organization that provides year-round sports training and competition for children and adults with intellectual disabilities. The Special Olympics was founded in 1968 by Eunice Kennedy Shriver. The Special Olympics offers a variety of sports, including athletics, swimming, basketball, and soccer. The organization also hosts a number of events throughout the year, including the Special Olympics World Games. The Special Olympics is committed to providing a positive and inclusive environment for athletes with intellectual disabilities. The organization believes that sports can help people with intellectual disabilities to develop their physical, social, and emotional skills.
